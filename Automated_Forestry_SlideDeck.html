Forests: Critical to Paris Agreement Goals

Enhancing Carbon Storage and Sequestration

  • Reliance on forests for CO2 emission reduction.
  • Forests as a vast carbon removal service (13+ billion tonnes CO2/year).
  • Regrowing forests as low-cost, large-scale CDR.

Uncertainty Threatens Forest Carbon Sinks

Climate Change & Human Intervention Impacts

  • Forests vulnerable to environmental changes (e.g., warmer temps, CO2 fertilization, phosphorus limitation).
  • Direct human activities (deforestation, degradation) underestimated in models.
  • Models might be too optimistic about future forest carbon storage.

Assessing Policy Responses to Forest Loss

REMIND-MAgPIE Model & Scenario Analysis

  • Uses IAM REMIND-MAgPIE model.
  • Introduces "stylized disturbance rates" (e.g., 0.4% per year).
  • Compares "Foresighted" (immediate action) vs. "Myopic" (delayed action) policies.

Key Finding: Delaying Action Doubles Mitigation Costs

Economic Burden of Reactive Policies

  • Myopic, 5-year delay: more than doubles additional mitigation costs and land use.
  • Myopic response to FCL is as stringent as foresighted response to twice the FCL rate.
  • Burden shifts to other sectors (energy, industry, transport).

Urgent Need for Proactive Forest Management

Safeguarding Climate Goals & Economic Feasibility

  • Climate policy must treat forest permanence as a significant risk.
  • Invest in monitoring and safeguarding forests.
  • Integrate realistic disturbance projections into climate models.

Driving Sustainable Impact

  • Proactive forest management is an economic imperative.
  • Integrated strategies are key to achieving Paris Agreement goals.
  • Let's collaborate for a resilient, decarbonized future.
